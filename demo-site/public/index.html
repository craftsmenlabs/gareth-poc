Why

We think that while creating software we make, technically and from a business perspective, assumptions on how the product will behave once in production. We also think that those assumptions need to be verified. And since we mostly build our products iterative, we need to be able to not only verify these assumptions once but multiple times over a period of time. “Validate your assumptions automically and make them part of your development proces”

How

“By describing assumptions in a structured language, we can automatically validate if the goals and assumptions that a product owner made on forehand are really reached.”

What

“We want to create a DSL that product owners can use to describe their assumptions for a user story. We want to validate these described assumptions by adding gluecode and running these assumptions in a framework that is available next to your application in production.”

1. Validate your ideas and businessgoals

  • The first round of validation is between the Product Owner, his or her stakeholders and is administered in the backlog, story maps and product canvasses.
  • Validations in this round are: Is the vision of the product still right? Are the right persona’s defined? Is the impact of the software defined? Are the right activities defined? Are the goals clear?...
  • The assumptions we make in this stage are things like: the performance of feature z will be 25% better, feature y will decrease the usage of certain functionality by at least 50% over the next 3 months, Feature x will be in our top 5 of highest value business transactions, after implementing feature f the use of the contactform will decrease by 10 per week. Don't worry! That’s where Gareth comes in. Just write the assumptions down in easy readable human language.

2. Technical & behavioural validation

  • The second round of validation is about the building, testing and accepting. Features are being designed, build and tested. After that it is up to the Product Owner to accept this functionality.
  • Validations in this round are things like: Is the behavior of the software as intended? Do all unit tests pass? Does the continuous integration builds pass? Does the software look the way the Product Owner intended it?...

3. Live validation

  • Hooray! Your software is on live! Let’s NOT sit back and relax. Let’s be sure that this is what the world really needs. It’s about getting proactive monitoring in place.
  • Gareth will run all assumptions which are scheduled and will display the result on a dashboard. Here the Product Owner and stakeholders can see if their assumptions hold up. Based on this information the backlog and roadmaps could be adjusted.

Get feedback

  • Feedback is one of the core features of Gareth. Gareth will alert you when an assumption is not right or is not right anymore due to a change in the software. These are the thing we think we can really benefit from. We are now able to decide to adjust the feature or maybe completely remove it based on evidence. We think that there is no shame in removing features that don’t give the resultswe where expecting.

Stories that don’t suck:

Good story describes the goal a certain user wants to achieve with a piece of functionality (the why). It should be pretty functional. It works very well to add acceptance criteria (BDD/ATDD scenarios) in an early stage to fine-tune and make sure a functional validation is in place. Let’s say that’s all in order. We now want to introduce another dimension to make sure we achieve our goals. We add something we called an experiment, which includes some assumptions, to an user story. These assumptions can be measured and will be automatically validated. Here is an example of a story we are using in our demo setup which contains both the BDD feature and an experiment:

User Story:

As a BusDevOps consultant,
I want a simple subscription form on my website,
So that I have the information of the people that want to know more about BusDevOps.

The behaviour:

Feature: Let people easily fill in their credentials and give them some feedback.

Scenario: Fill the BusDevOps form.

Given I am on the form page
When I fill in my name
And I fill in my email address
And I select the submit button
Then the message "submission accepted" should be displayed

Validations:

Experiment: I want to validate the amount of expected use of the subscription form. I think this subject is very popular and will give me world fame.

Baseline: Get the current filled in forms.
Assumption: There will be at least an increase of 80 filled forms in the first 2 months.
Baseline: Get the current filled in forms per month.
Assumption: There will be at least 25 filled forms every month for the next 12 months.
Baseline: Get the current amount of calls to the info number.
Assumption: There will be at least an minimum of 15 calls about BusDevOps per month the next 6 months.
